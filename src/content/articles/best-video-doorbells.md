## How we chose

A video doorbell has one job: to reliably tell you who's at your door and let you
respond, day or night. The best ones do that with sharp video, alerts that
arrive in time to matter, and without quietly forcing you into an expensive
subscription. We judged each on exactly those points, plus how easily it fits an
ordinary home.

## What matters when you buy

### The subscription question comes first

More than anything else, understand the **running cost** before you buy. Some
doorbells store footage locally for free; others make core features — even
seeing your recorded clips — dependent on a monthly plan. Neither approach is
wrong, but you should choose it deliberately rather than discover it later.

### A tall view beats a big megapixel number

Marketing loves resolution figures, but the more useful specification is the
**shape** of the view. A tall, head-to-toe field of view shows a visitor's face
and any parcel left on the ground. A wide-but-short view misses the doorstep
where packages actually sit.

### Battery or wired

**Battery** doorbells install in minutes and suit renters or homes without
existing doorbell wiring — you just recharge them periodically. **Wired** models
draw constant power, so they can record continuously and never go flat, but need
your existing chime wiring and a slightly more involved fit.

### Match it to your world

Video doorbells are happiest inside an ecosystem. If you use a particular phone,
voice assistant or smart display, a doorbell from the same family will answer on
those screens and play nicely with the rest of your devices. It's a small thing
that makes daily use far smoother.

## Who should buy which

- **Most people:** the best overall pick keeps things simple and compatible, and
  its subscription is optional for the basics.
- **Fee-avoiders:** choose the subscription-free pick with free local storage and
  never pay a monthly cost.
- **Google households:** the Google-friendly pick slots neatly into Android
  phones and Nest displays.

Whichever you choose, install it at the right height, point it to cover your
doorstep, and decide your storage plan up front — do that and a video doorbell is
one of the most reassuring upgrades you can make.
