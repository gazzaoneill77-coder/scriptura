## The short version

Roborock and eufy are the two names most people end up choosing between, and for
good reason — both make dependable robot vacuums that navigate well and last.
The decision comes down to temperament. **Roborock** is the more refined
all-rounder, with the most consistent navigation and the best mopping across its
range. **eufy** is the value-focused specialist, giving you more hardware for
your money and particularly strong pet-hair handling.

## Navigation

Both brands have standardised on LiDAR across their mid-range and up, so both
map accurately and clean in tidy rows even in the dark. In our experience
Roborock's mapping and obstacle handling feel a touch more polished from model to
model, while eufy's is very good but occasionally less consistent on the most
complex layouts. For most homes, either is more than good enough.

## Pet hair

This is eufy's calling card. Its anti-tangle brush designs are consistently
excellent at shedding pet hair rather than wrapping it, which keeps the robot
cleaning without you unpicking a knotted roller each week. Roborock's premium
models handle pets well too, but if pets are your single biggest concern, eufy
is the more reliable bet across price points.

## Mopping

If mopping matters, Roborock generally leads. Its higher-end robots offer the
most effective mopping — including self-washing pads — and the results on sealed
hard floors are the best we've tested. eufy's mopping is good and improving, but
Roborock sets the pace here.

## Value

Pound for pound, eufy tends to give you more: a self-emptying base or stronger
suction at a price where Roborock offers refinement instead. Neither is "cheap,"
but eufy more often feels like the better deal, while Roborock asks a small
premium for its extra polish.

## Which should you buy?

- **Buy Roborock** if you want the most polished all-round experience, the best
  mopping, and advanced app control — and you'll pay a little more for it.
- **Buy eufy** if you want the best pet-hair handling and the most hardware for
  your money, with an app that keeps things simple.

You won't go wrong with either. Decide which of the two things you value more —
all-round refinement or targeted value — and the choice makes itself.
